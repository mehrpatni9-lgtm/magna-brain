MAGNA PRINCIPLE #012: "The turning point isn't when the business changed. It's when you didn't."

Every founder I've sat with — at $10M, at $50M, at the exit table — can name their revenue inflections. Those aren't the turning points.

The real turning point happens months earlier, quietly. The business starts asking the founder to become a different operator. Most founders don't hear the ask. They keep running the company they built instead of the company it's becoming.

Creation rewards knowing everything. Acceleration punishes it.

At $10M, you're the bottleneck and the hero. At $50M, being the bottleneck is the problem you can't outwork. The shift isn't tactical — it's identity. You stop being the person who has every answer and start being the person who builds the room that finds answers without you.

The founders who break through aren't the ones who scaled faster. They're the ones who let go of the version of themselves that got them there.

Your business will outlast every version of you that built it. The turning point is the one where you let that version go first.
