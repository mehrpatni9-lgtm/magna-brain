01
On Negotiation
"
You can never convince somebody of anything. You have to push an open door.
Sylvie spent 20 years in finance before building Lynx.

Negotiation isn't force. It's finding the door that's already moving.
Image direction: A hand gently pushing an ajar door — warm light behind it, editorial feel
MAGNA
02
On Leadership
"
The founder job and the CEO job are two completely different things.
One is vision — where the company is going, raising capital, seeing around corners.
The other is operations — running the day-to-day, building the team.

Most founders realise too late they need to grow into both.
Image direction: Split or duality visual — horizon view on one side, a focused desk/meeting on the other
MAGNA
03
On Pivoting
"
The train was going fast — but in the wrong direction.
Two years into building Lynx, funding didn't come and clients didn't close.
She cut the budget, changed the team, and flew to Malta for a full reset.

When she came back, revenue doubled.
Image direction: A train at a junction point — dramatic, moody lighting, sense of a decision
MAGNA
04
On Hard Decisions
"
I brought the whole company onto one call. I shared everything.
How much money was in the bank. Who was leaving and why. What happened next.

Fast decision. Total clarity. No ambiguity.
That's how you lead through a hard moment.
Image direction: A single figure standing before a small group — honest, open body language, warm but serious tone
MAGNA
05
On Support
"
I thought my network was good enough. I needed 5–10x more people — and different kinds.
Fellow founders. VCs. Mentors. Corporate friends. Business school alumni.

The depth and diversity of your support system is a competitive advantage.
Build it before you need it.
Image direction: Expanding web of connections — organic, warm, not corporate — think dinner table not org chart
MAGNA
06
On Delegation
"
When it's your own company, letting go is much harder than I expected.
She managed teams of hundreds across Latin America and Europe.
But in her own company, the emotional attachment changes everything.

The skill isn't delegation. It's learning to trust what you built.
Image direction: Open hands releasing — soft light, intimate, human — not dramatic
