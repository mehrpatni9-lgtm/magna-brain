# Content strategy instructions — April 29 2026

## Series changes
- DROP: Founder Fridays (IG Friday) — removed from rotation
- DROP: Inspiration Sundays (IG Sunday) — removed from rotation
- ACTIVE IG series: Magna Mondays (Monday), Wisdom Wednesdays (Wednesday)
- ACTIVE LI series: The Principle (Tuesday), The Sharp Take (Thursday), Digital Lectures (Saturday A), Behind the Build (Saturday B alt)

## Content direction
- No fake or invented company insights. Zero internal company updates without real sourcing.
- Every factual claim must have a named source (publication, report, data provider).
- Content should be: informational, storytelling-led, market research-based, savable, real-time updated.
- Format priority: carousel (most savable), then single insight post, then video clip.

## The three arms — must stay balanced across week and month
- Academy: how to think — frameworks, methodology, mental models
- Strategy: what to do — advisory clarity, decision-making, inflection points
- Capital: where the money is — market intelligence, investment landscape, deal signals
- No arm should exceed 40% of monthly content.

## Geographies Magna serves — content must rotate across these
- USA: HQ. Chicago base. Audience: $10M-$50M founders, PE-adjacent executives, Midwest operators.
- UAE: Dubai office. Audience: GCC founders, family office executives, MENA investors, cross-border deal-makers.
- India — four cities:
  - Bangalore: tech founders, SaaS, deep tech, 400K+ software engineers, largest VC concentration in India
  - Mumbai: fintech, insurtech, traditional business families entering venture, proximity to RBI/SEBI
  - Hyderabad: pharma, biotech, IT services
  - Chennai: manufacturing, IT services, traditional business

## Audience mindset by geography
- USA founders: scaling past $10M, navigating capital markets, AI impact on operations, PE exit readiness
- UAE/MENA: cross-border deal flow, family office allocation, regional VC landscape, MENA growth story
- India: fundraising in a selective market, cross-border expansion to UAE/USA, India Stack opportunity, domestic vs global build strategy
