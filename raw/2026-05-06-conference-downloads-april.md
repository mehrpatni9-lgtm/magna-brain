## 3. Mexico HBS — Economic Overview
**Date:** April 16, 2026 | **Location:** Mexico City
**Speakers:** Carlos Elizondo (economics/politics), Enrique Madrid (former tourism minister), Andrey Zanur (biotech entrepreneur)
**Magna attendees to tag:** Temo Mikalzade, Robert Rodriguez, Javier Marin, Tahsin Khan, Shailu Tipparaju

**Top takeaways:**
- Mexico is a low-growth economy, but consumption is growing faster than GDP — driven by wage increases and remittances. Plan for demand from higher pay, not GDP growth.
- 82% of Mexico's exports go to the US — the most US-integrated economy in the world. US policy changes have outsized impact.
- Private investment has gone slightly down. Public projects are poorly designed (train, airport, refinery cited as examples).
- Political/legal system overhauled: Supreme Court members fired, half of federal judges replaced. Legal and regulatory rules are less predictable.
- 40% of active pharmaceutical ingredients come from China. 100% of key starting materials for agriculture come from China. Nearshoring alone doesn't solve upstream input dependence.
- Energy and constitutional policy shifts are big constraints on investment.

**Key insight for content:** "Nearshoring alone doesn't solve dependence on upstream inputs." — The supply chain conversation is more complex than most founders realize. Proximity is not the same as independence.

## 4. Global Leadership Conference (GLC)
**Date:** April 28, 2026 | **Location:** Chicago
**Scale:** 1,600+ world leaders. Shailu and the Chicago team were the hospitality crew.

**Core framework from conference:**
Leadership = Humanity × Resilience × Reinvention × Peer Intelligence

**Four pillars distilled:**

**1. Leadership → Human-Centered Stewardship**
The center of gravity has moved. Leadership is no longer defined by scale, control, or performance optics. It is defined by depth, humanity, and responsibility. "Lead with humanity" was the operating tone. Awe, beauty, reflection — positioned as strategic enablers, not indulgences.
Implication: The leaders who win the next decade will be those who can hold intensity and introspection simultaneously.

**2. CEO → Chief Resilience Officer of the Polycrisis**
The external environment is no longer linear — it's layered volatility. Geopolitics. Technology disruption. Climate stress. A constant state of polycrisis. Systems must absorb shocks, not just optimize outputs. Decision-making must function under incomplete and shifting data.
Reframe: Strategy is no longer about efficiency. It is resilience engineering under uncertainty.

**3. Competitive Edge → Velocity + Reinvention**
Static advantage is dead. Markets are moving too fast for "defensible positions" to hold for long. Reinvention is continuous, not episodic. Speed of adaptation > strength of original strategy.
Truth: Your edge is your willingness to rebuild — again and again — before the market forces you to.

**4. Value of YPO → Peer Truth Exchange**
Not keynotes. Not frameworks. Unfiltered operator conversations. Deep trust rooms > stage content. Lived experience > theoretical insight. Vulnerability accelerates learning cycles.
Highest ROI room: Operators speaking honestly about what's breaking — not what's working.
