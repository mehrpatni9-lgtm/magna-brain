# LinkedIn Caption — HBS Fireside Chat, Chicago

**Event:** Harvard Business School Executive Education fireside chat with Professor Deepak Malhotra (OPM faculty chair) and OPM alumnus Shailu Tipparaju. Chicago alumni gathering.

**Topics discussed:** How global companies are navigating current political and economic headwinds; the impact of AI on the future of business.

---

## Draft Caption (Shailu's LinkedIn)

The room was full of executives and entrepreneurs.

The conversation was about two forces reshaping every business decision right now: geopolitical headwinds and AI.

Professor Deepak Malhotra — Harvard Business School faculty chair of the OPM program — put it simply: the leaders who will come out of this period ahead are not the ones who react fastest. They're the ones who hold clarity longest under pressure.

Three things stayed with me from the evening:

**1. Uncertainty is not a reason to pause — it's a reason to decide with better frameworks.**
Political and economic headwinds don't wait for confidence. The question isn't "when does it stabilize?" It's "what do I know right now that's true regardless of what happens next?"

**2. AI is not a productivity tool. It's a strategy question.**
The companies getting it wrong are optimizing workflows. The companies getting it right are asking: if this technology removes our current competitive advantage, what do we need to be building today?

**3. The best rooms are the ones where the thinking changes you — not just informs you.**
That was this evening. Thank you to Harvard Business School Executive Education and Ani Kharajian for bringing the Crimson Corridor to Chicago. And to every executive, founder, and community leader who showed up with genuine intellectual curiosity.

The conversation continues. It always should.
