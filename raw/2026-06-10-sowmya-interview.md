## [voice] Sowmya Iyer — Founder Fridays interview transcript — 15:20

Recorded interview (~25 min). Cleaned transcript below; ASR mishears corrected (DViO, Simon Sinek, OPM, names). Mid-session logistics call trimmed.

**On "marketing that doesn't look like marketing":** It's a pull versus push concept. When a brand stands for something and people resonate with it, they come to the brand — that's pull, marketing that doesn't feel like marketing. The moment a brand pushes itself in your face, you get skeptics and credibility questions. What you stand for, how you blend in, and how useful you are in people's daily lives decides whether you attract people or have to sell yourself.

**On her edge after 20+ years:** Started at 16 running a .com portal. Has watched tech evolve fast. The last 18 months of AI have made her last 13 years feel slow — but she's more prepared, while it's a shock to many companies. Experience taught her to adapt and adopt new tech. "Everybody is in this rut that we need to catch up" — she isn't shocked by change because she's been tuned to it for years.

**On what's broken in the traditional agency model:** It bills hours, doesn't understand outcomes, is people-driven. But search, buying, and customer understanding have all changed forever. Marketing has to be system-led, not campaign-led. DViO transformed from a digital marketing company into a growth operating system — "systems, not services." With data she can design an entire experience around a customer; not using that leaves value on the table. Needs a system that identifies the customer and lets automation talk to the brand.

**On the 2017 buyout:** Cost a lot of money but taught her to believe in an entrepreneur's gut. Didn't come from a business background; mortgaged the house she lived in to buy out her partners. Every consultant told her not to. "Something unique told me to do it" — defied logic. When conviction comes from authenticity, you bet everything behind it. Believing her gut and calling is her biggest learning.

**On leadership (rules):** Big fan of Simon Sinek. "Leaders eat last." They inspire and see more potential in their people than the people see in themselves — so seeing and unlocking potential is paramount. Second: a clear vision — "one leg in the future, one leg in the present." Both legs in the future and you lose access to the people you're leading; start from where they are. Leaders eat last = putting the higher purpose ahead of self.

**On Harvard OPM:** "We're the sum total of the people we surround ourselves with." Entrepreneurs become a frog in the well, limited by their vision. OPM was to expand that vision — meet entrepreneurs doing bigger things, understand how they think, gain exposure beyond her operating countries, so DViO's impact multiplies. "Increase your surface area for opportunities to come."

**On being a woman in agency ownership:** Almost infamous for her comments in women's entrepreneurial circles. Her gender never occurred to her while building — the world rewarded her for meritocracy. Hasn't raised capital; everything self-funded. Never faced discrimination, even in a conservative GCC market (running there since 2015). One client is a Sharia-compliant company with a board of traditional dishdasha-clad men; she presented the future of marketing to them 10 years ago and they're still clients. "Women tend to identify so much with their own gender that they ruin it for themselves." Has taken public backlash; acknowledges real upliftment needed in tier-2/3 and rural India, but in advertising/media/martech/AI she's "a woman-run company with no client ever having discriminated against me."

**On upbringing (Tamil Brahmin):** Education is highly valued — even marriage is secondary; no discrimination between educating a girl or a boy. Cites Indra Nooyi and TCS chairman Chandrasekaran as Tamil Brahmins. That value of educating yourself brought her back to academia at Harvard. Her mother is a B.A. in economics, was financially independent, supported her family for 10 years, and married at 33 — "unheard of." "It comes from there. Generation after generation."

**Superpower wish:** To be in multiple places at once. Comes from an honest place — has to be in the US, Dubai, and India. "You get so much more done when you're physically present somewhere."

**This or That:**

- Solo founder or co-founder → **Solo founder** ("I do best on my own")
- Investor meeting or client dinner → **Client**
