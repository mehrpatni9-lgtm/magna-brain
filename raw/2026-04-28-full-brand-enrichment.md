## Brand Voice (from Brand Guidelines)

**Core feeling:** Quiet confidence. Depth of thought. Enduring credibility.
**Voice in three words:** Authoritative. Precise. Grounded.

**DO:**
- Precise, data-anchored statements
- Strategic, long-horizon framing
- Calm authority — let the insight do the work
- Global perspective, specific examples
- Numbers and specifics that earn trust

**DON'T:**
- Vague promises or buzzword soup
- Hype language ("game-changer", "revolutionary")
- Loud or urgent sales tone
- Generic market commentary
- Clickbait hooks or emotional manipulation

**Writing style:**
- Short, declarative sentences for key points
- Data and specifics always preferred over adjectives
- No exclamation marks in professional contexts
- Avoid first-person plural ("we") — lead with the insight
- No emojis used decoratively (functional use sparingly)

## Workshop Teaching Content (from lecture transcripts)

### Strategy Workshop
- Business DNA framework: Customer needs → Value delivery → Channels → Pricing → Mission/Meaning
- "Magnet Triangle": left = customer needs/goals, middle = value delivery, right = channels, bottom = pricing
- Strategy = the choices you're making and how you communicate them (to team, market, ecosystem — in that order)
- Matrix question: few things for many vs. many things for few vs. many for many → decides pricing strategy
- "An organization without strategy is just guessing"
- Key frameworks: value proposition, key differentiator, value creation process, value capture, strategic choices, adaptable elements

### Negotiation Workshop
- "Negotiation is not a game where somebody wins and somebody loses — it's a process"
- Principles-based approach (not rules or laws)
- Humanize the process — you're dealing with people in a company, not the company
- Superpowers of negotiation: clarity of preparation, clarity of assumptions, expected outcomes prediction
- Both sides walk out gaining something (win-win framing)
- Practical applications: selling companies, scaling, partner deals, ecosystem relationships, investor negotiations
- Common failure: overconfidence entering negotiation; preparation is the real superpower

### Sales / Marketing / Branding Workshop
- "The Magna world of sales does not teach sales. It enables buying."
- Create environment of trust, respect, value, clarity → the paperwork is the final transaction
- Sales analogy: "Asking out = sale. Reason they say yes = branding. Setting the environment = marketing"
- The outer sphere: Identity → Experience → Engagement (like sugar in coffee — felt but not seen)
- Inner triangle: Strategy → Design → Communication (in frequency of consumer)
- Key insight: 75,000 individual data footprints captured per customer (by 2017)
- Magna's purpose: bridging world's leading educators/researchers with entrepreneurs/executives
