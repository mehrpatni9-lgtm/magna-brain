## The angle

Magna's actual product is clarity. Every lecture, every framework, every roundtable — they all exist to resolve one thing: *which problem is actually the problem*. Frameworks are cheap. Clarity on where to apply them is rare.

## Evidence trail

- [[ig-magna-mondays-004-thinking-problem]] states it directly: *"Most founders don't have a strategy problem. They have a thinking problem."*
- [[magna-pillar-strategy]] frames strategy-as-clarity as the first pillar, not strategy-as-plan.
- [[voice/styleguide]]'s worldview line: *"Strategy is easy to buy. Clarity on which problem is actually the problem — that's the hard part."*
- The entire [[tone-compass]] is built to deliver distinctions with surgical specificity — every voice is a different mode of *sharpening*.
- [[magna-audience]] screenshots posts *before 9am* because they're reaching for a distinction they can carry into that day's decision.

## Counter-angle

The counter-claim: founders don't *need* more clarity; they need more *execution*. Hustle-culture voices argue that thinking is the escape from doing. Magna's position on this is already in [[li-sharp-take-001-hustle]] — hustle culture is a distraction strategy. But the counter-claim is still worth naming explicitly in any post that uses this angle: *yes, there are founders who overthink; this isn't about them.*

## Post hooks

- "You don't have a strategy problem. You have a thinking problem — and you've been buying frameworks to avoid it."
- "Clarity is the actual product. Everything else is packaging."
- "The framework is cheap. Knowing which problem it's for is the whole job."
- "Most founders I work with have three strategies running in parallel. None of them is the right one."
- "The $10M founder buys frameworks. The $50M founder buys hours to think."
