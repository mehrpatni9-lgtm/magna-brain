**TL;DR:** Shailu's framework for what a founder must personally manage — **Content · Community · Calendar.** Distinct from Magna's product-level 3 C's (Compass · Connections · Collaborations) — see [[magna-3cs]]. Do not conflate.

## Why it matters for Magna

Most founder advice focuses on the company. This framework focuses on the founder *as a unit of allocation* — what they themselves must produce, build, and protect. It implicitly argues that founder burnout, founder under-leverage, and founder visibility problems all collapse into mismanagement of these three.

For content, the framework gives Magna a recurring lens for "things founders forget to run themselves through" — calendar audits, community capital reviews, content stewardship.

## The three C's

- **Content.** The value the founder produces. Not posts — the *output of the founder's thinking* the world can see, learn from, or buy.
- **Community.** Building relationships through community capital and favours. Not just network — accumulated trust earned by showing up.
- **Calendar.** Disciplined management of the most limited resource — time. Calendar reflects priority more honestly than any stated strategy.

## Important distinction (again)

Magna has TWO different 3 C's:

- **Product-level**: Compass · Connections · Collaborations — see [[magna-3cs]]. *What Magna sells.*
- **Founder-level** (this one): Content · Community · Calendar. *What founders manage themselves.*

Do not conflate. Posts that reference "Magna's 3 C's" without specifying which set will read confused. [[strategic-investment-readiness-workshop]]
