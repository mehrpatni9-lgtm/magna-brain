**TL;DR:** Shailu's strategy-as-DNA framework. A sequenced triangle — Customer Needs → Value Addition → Channels → Pricing → Mission. The order matters: Customer Needs first, always. Pricing is a strategy decision, not a final-step afterthought.

## Why it matters for Magna

Most strategy frameworks are about positioning vs competitors. The Magnet Triangle starts inside the customer and works outward. It also names something most founders skip: **pricing as strategy upfront**, not pricing as a calculation later.

The framework gives Magna a clear teaching pattern: *if any one of these five is missing or out of order, scaling will fail in a specific way.*

## The five components (sequenced)

1. **Customer Needs.** Start here, always. Goals and ambitions of the customer — not the product's features.
2. **Value Addition.** How do you add value on the *customer's* terms, given what you know about their needs?
3. **Channels.** How do you deliver that value the way the customer finds most efficient?
4. **Pricing.** How do you price it so the business creates profit? *Pricing is strategy, not an afterthought.*
5. **Mission and Meaning.** The north star that holds the triangle together.

## Verbatim Shailu

> *"If you do not make a pricing strategy upfront, you may be scaling your product too fast and end up delivering with no profit resulting in a pretty tough situation as we get to scale."* [[shailu-live-workshop-transcripts]]
