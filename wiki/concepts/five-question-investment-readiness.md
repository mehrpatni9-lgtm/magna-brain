**TL;DR:** Shailu's pre-raise checklist. Five questions a founder must answer honestly before walking into an investor meeting. If they cannot answer all five, they are not ready to raise. This is not a soft self-assessment — it is the gate.

## Why it matters for Magna

This framework is one of Magna's most concrete tactical exercises. It exemplifies a Shailu teaching pattern (see [[shailu-live-workshop-transcripts]]): *the exercise is the method.* Shailu doesn't just teach principles; he gives founders something to do that produces the thinking shift.

For content writing, the framework is a strong carousel format (one question per slide) and a strong long-form post (each question expanded with the trap it catches).

## The five questions

1. **Scaling Potential.** Can this product grow exponentially, or is it merely a linear service business?
2. **Vision.** What is the specific, long-term destination 5–10 years out?
3. **Numbers.** Authentic growth data and specific KPIs. Move far beyond *"vanity metrics"* sophisticated investors see through instantly.
4. **Valuation.** Realistic worth based on **market comparables**, not founder ego.
5. **Process.** A documented roadmap for how the raise will be executed — technically and strategically.

## The sharp distinctions inside the framework

- **Vanity metrics vs authentic data** — the cleanest content hook in this framework.
- **Founder ego valuation vs market comparable valuation** — a practical reframe most fundraising content avoids.
