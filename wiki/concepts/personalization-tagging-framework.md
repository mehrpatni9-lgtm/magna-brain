**TL;DR:** Every Magna post tags **five dimensions**: individual + company + industry + geography + area of interest. Bulk tagging is dead. The post must benefit the tagged contact *and* Magna's outreach simultaneously — if it only benefits Magna, it's a broadcast and won't engage.

## Why it matters for Magna

This is how content stops being content and starts being *outreach with public proof*. A post tagged this way does three jobs in one shot:

1. The tagged founder sees a piece of public recognition that's specific to them — shareable, screenshot-worthy, ego-aligned.
2. Magna's network sees a credible name/company/sector pairing — proof of relevance.
3. Magna's CRM gets a touchpoint that's already done the hard work of being interesting *before* the follow-up email goes out.

This replaces the brand's old default (broad-strokes thought leadership posts that anyone could've written) with a model where every post is also a relationship asset.

## The five tags

| Tag | What it pins down |
|---|---|
| **Individual** | The named founder/operator the post is about (or implicitly about). Real human. Not a persona. |
| **Company** | The specific business — sector positioning visible at a glance. |
| **Industry** | One of the 33 predefined industries from the [[magna-compass]] list. |
| **Geography** | City, not country. Dubai, California, Hyderabad, São Paulo — not "Middle East" or "USA". |
| **Area of interest** | The Magna pillar or theme it ladders to: [[magna-pillar-strategy]], [[magna-pillar-negotiation]], [[magna-pillar-capital]], [[magna-pillar-culture]], [[magna-pillar-leadership]] — or a specific framework like [[four-dimensions-of-wealth]] or [[mindset-of-leverage]]. |

## The benefit-symmetry rule

A post is publishable only if it gives the tagged contact something *they* would proudly share — not just something Magna wants to say *about* them. Practical check: would this person retweet/repost it themselves?

If the answer is "no" or "maybe" — the post is broadcast, not personalization.
