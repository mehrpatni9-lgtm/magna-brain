**TL;DR:** Every Magna customer/prospect is sorted into one of three buckets — **Economic, Strategy, or Capital** — and each bucket maps to a different engagement model, content register, and sales motion. Soman's May prioritization task is to label all 109 community members against this taxonomy.

## Why it matters for Magna

This replaces "everyone is a community member" with a triage that lets the team *act differently* on different relationships. It's the operational layer that makes the [[positioning-shift-strategy-firm]] runnable: if strategy consulting is the lead offering, then the *strategy* bucket is who buys it, and the other two buckets need their own engagement model that doesn't try to force a strategy-consulting close.

## The three buckets

### 1. Economic

Customers/prospects whose engagement is primarily transactional — they're buying or considering a paid Magna offering (cohort seat, advisory engagement, event ticket). Engagement model: clear pricing, clear deliverable, clear ROI conversation.

### 2. Strategy

Customers/prospects who come to Magna for thinking — clarity on which problem is the problem, framework selection, decision support. This is where the lead offering ([[positioning-shift-strategy-firm]]) sells. Engagement model: discovery → diagnostic → engagement.

### 3. Capital

Customers/prospects on either side of the capital flow — founders raising, investors deploying, or strategic acquirers exploring. Magna's edge here is corridor knowledge and the [[magna-capital]] thesis (merit · market · maturity). Engagement model: introductions, deal context, capital-strategy conversations.

## How the buckets get used

- **Soman's prioritization task** — label all 109 community members.
- **Connect module** — the bucket becomes a filterable field; meeting prep can pull "all Strategy bucket contacts in California" in one query.
- **Outreach calendar** — a Capital-bucket contact gets a different email than a Strategy-bucket one; Mehr's templates need three variants.
- **Content tagging** — when a post tags a specific contact (see [[personalization-tagging-framework]]), the bucket informs the angle.

## Relationship to capital-delivery rule

Buckets are about *who* the customer is. The four-value capital-delivery rule — every contract must deliver Social, Emotional, Intellectual, or Financial value (see [[four-dimensions-of-wealth]]) — is about *what* the contract delivers. The two combine: an Economic-bucket contract typically leads with Financial value; a Strategy-bucket contract typically leads with Intellectual; a Capital-bucket contract usually combines Social (network) + Financial. The bucket predicts which of the four dimensions will be load-bearing.
