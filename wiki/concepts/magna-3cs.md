**TL;DR:** Magna's three solution pillars — how the value gets delivered. **Compass** (frameworks/intelligence) · **Connections** (network/community) · **Collaborations** (workshops and partner execution). Distinct from the founder-balance 3 C's (Content/Community/Calendar) — see [[three-cs-founder-balance]].

## Why it matters for Magna

The 3 C's are how Magna explains itself to a buyer in one breath. Each C is a layer:

- **Compass** = intellectual foundation (the worldview).
- **Connections** = relationship layer (the cohort, the network).
- **Collaborations** = application layer (where frameworks meet real decisions).

A complete Magna engagement touches all three. Pulling out any one weakens the package.

## The three pillars

### 1. Magna Compass

Research-based learning frameworks. Curated content library from education and industry experts. The intellectual foundation — the global industry blueprint that gives founders the worldview of institutional investors. See [[magna-compass]].

### 2. Magna Connections

Platform blending learning cohorts and Magna networks. Personalised Magna Concierge support. The relationship layer — small, diverse, trusted peer groups of senior executives who understand your challenges without being in your organisation.

### 3. Magna Collaborations

High-impact workshops (1-1 and 1-many) on Creation, Acceleration, and Transition phases. Magna partners executing projects with measurable growth output. The application layer — where frameworks meet real decisions.

## Important distinction

Magna has **two different 3 C's** — they are NOT the same:

- **Product-level 3 C's** (this concept): Compass · Connections · Collaborations.
- **Founder-level 3 C's** ([[three-cs-founder-balance]]): Content · Community · Calendar.

Do not conflate them. The first is what Magna sells; the second is what Magna teaches founders to manage themselves. [[strategic-investment-readiness-workshop]]
