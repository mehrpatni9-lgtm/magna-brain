**TL;DR:** Magna's three-phase business lifecycle — **Creation** (inception/origination), **Acceleration** (growth/scale), **Transition** (succession/exit). The organising frame for when and how different strategies apply. Used live in Magna workshops as the master sequence.

## Why it matters for Magna

This is the time-axis the entire Magna curriculum sits on. Every program (Magna Academy, Magna Meetup, Magna Capital) maps to one or more phases. Every strategy concept Shailu teaches gets timed against where the founder is in the sequence.

For content writing, this framework is a powerful series spine: *"the strategy mistake founders make in Creation that haunts them in Transition."* / *"why Acceleration is the riskiest phase for [thing]."*

## The three phases

- **Creation** — inception. Self-awareness, idea origination, founding. The phase where identity and intent get set.
- **Acceleration** — growth and scale. Operations, team, market expansion. The phase where most companies break.
- **Transition** — succession or exit. Legacy, handover, next chapter. The phase most founders prepare for badly because they prepared for it not at all.

## Verbatim Shailu (live workshop)

> *"Companies at any stage are at a creation phase, at an acceleration growth phase, or at a succession or transition phase. A company is no different than a human. They go through phases of growth, evolution, maturity and decline."* [[shailu-live-workshop-transcripts]]
