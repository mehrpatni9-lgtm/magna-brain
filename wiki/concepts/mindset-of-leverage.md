**TL;DR:** Shailu's foundational fundraising principle — *seek capital when you do not need it.* Power lives entirely in the moment before you need the deal. Approaching investors out of desperation = already lost.

## Why it matters for Magna

This is one of the most postable Shailu frameworks because it inverts conventional founder behaviour and is true. Most founders raise when they're running out of runway — Shailu's claim is that this is the worst possible time to raise, because every leverage advantage is gone the moment the founder is "thirsty."

Maps directly to [[magna-pillar-capital]] and [[magna-pillar-negotiation]] — capital and negotiation share the same root principle: walkaway power is the actual product.

## Verbatim Shailu

> *"The golden rule of fundraising is simple: Seek investment when you do not need it. If you approach a firm out of desperation, you have already lost."* [[strategic-investment-readiness-workshop]]

## The "thirsty" framing

Shailu's word for the desperate state is **thirsty**. Specific, visual, slightly sharp. The mindset of leverage is the opposite — the founder who has options, time, and alternatives. Thirsty founders make catastrophic equity decisions. Non-thirsty founders make leverage decisions.

## Implications for content

- "Seek investment when you don't need it" is a one-liner that lands on its own.
- Pairs naturally with [[five-question-investment-readiness]] — the framework forces the founder to confront whether they're entering the room from leverage or from need.
- Connects to negotiation: *"You don't need to finish the deal all the time"* — see [[superpowers-of-negotiation]]. Both are expressions of the same underlying principle.
