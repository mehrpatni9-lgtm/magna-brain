**TL;DR:** Magna's proprietary pedagogy. Three pillars — Participation, Distillation, Application — designed to be distinct from standard MBA pedagogy. The "how" of how Magna teaches.

## Why it matters for Magna

Hyper-Learning is the reason a Magna cohort is supposed to land differently from a generic MBA case-method class. It is also the reason Magna can claim an "Applied-MBA" register — every concept is experienced (Participation), reflected on (Distillation), and put to work (Application) inside the founder's actual business.

For content writing, this framework gives Magna content its action bias: most posts should leave the reader with something to *do* with their reflection, not just a thought to admire.

## The three pillars

### Pillar 1 — Participation

- Learning mindset: psychological preparation and intrinsic motivation.
- Environment setup: preparation for learning (environment, planning, systems).
- Focus: ensuring time allocated is not distracted by things that *seem* urgent.

### Pillar 2 — Distillation

- Associating the learning framework and networks to ongoing and future initiatives.
- Active reflection and **"inking the thinking"** — see [[think-in-ink]].

### Pillar 3 — Application

- Applied execution of frameworks; sharing knowledge with colleagues and teams.
- Cross-pollination with cohorts; finding avenues for shared growth and fulfilment.
