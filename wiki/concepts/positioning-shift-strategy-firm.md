**TL;DR:** The May 2026 repositioning. Magna stops describing itself as an academic learning platform and starts describing itself as a **strategy firm that helps founders scale revenue and find exits.** Driven by direct CEO feedback that entrepreneurs want scaling + investor help, not general learning.

## Why it matters for Magna

This is the single largest positioning decision of the year so far. It rewrites:

- Website CTAs (remove "learn / learning" framing; add "scale / scaling" and "exits" framing)
- Email templates (lead with strategy consulting, not curriculum)
- Content register (less academic explanation, more practitioner intelligence)
- Lead offering (**strategy consulting** moves to the front — highest customer appetite of all Magna offerings)
- Testimonial selection (currently engaged founders > legacy academic-content adopters)

It does NOT rewrite the underlying intellectual stack. The [[magna-compass]], [[four-dimensions-of-wealth]], [[quantum-worldview]], and the five [[magna-pillar-strategy]]/[[magna-pillar-negotiation]]/[[magna-pillar-capital]]/[[magna-pillar-culture]]/[[magna-pillar-leadership]] pillars stay intact. What changes is the *surface* — how Magna *names* what it does to the customer.

## The two customer needs (per CEO feedback)

1. **Scaling revenue** — how do I grow this company?
2. **Finding exits / investors** — how do I get capital or get out?

These two questions are the entire commercial demand surface. Anything Magna says publicly should land on one of them within two sentences.

## What this rules OUT in copy

- "Learn from a 25-year practitioner" — too academic
- "Curriculum / cohort / academy" as the lead noun
- General-learning CTAs ("Join our community to learn about strategy")
- Education-platform framing on hero pages

## What this rules IN

- "Scale your company" / "Find your next round" / "Plan your exit"
- Strategy consulting as the headline service
- Testimonials about *outcomes* (revenue moves, capital raised, exits) over testimonials about *insights gained*
- CTAs that tie a specific problem (scale or exit) to a specific Magna engagement

## Contradictions / open threads

- The [[magna-academy]] entity (10-week applied-MBA cohort) and the YouTube academy channel still exist as products. The shift isn't to kill them — it's to demote "academy" as the headline noun. Surface tension to monitor in copy: how does Magna talk about the cohort *without* leading with academic framing? Likely answer: as an outcome ("scaled to $30M after Quantum Leap") rather than a curriculum ("our 10-week program covers...").
- Existing testimonials (Martha, Ryan, Sriram) skewed academic. Replacement testimonials must come from currently engaged scaling/exit-stage founders to make the new framing credible. The testimonial swap *is* the positioning shift's first proof.
