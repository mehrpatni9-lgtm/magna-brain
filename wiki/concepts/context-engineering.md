**TL;DR:** Shailu's framing of how to use AI tools well — *"Tools like Claude are like having an intern with Einstein-level intelligence but zero institutional memory. If the output is poor, it is because your context was poor."* AI rewards specificity in context, not specificity in prompt. The skill is context engineering.

## Why it matters for Magna

This is a content-rich angle that lets Magna speak intelligently about AI without sounding like another AI-hype account. Shailu teaches AI not as a threat or a tool — but as a context problem. The output is downstream of how well you've onboarded the intern.

It also shows Magna's curriculum is AI-forward in a non-shallow way. Most exec-ed programs treat AI as either a feature or a fear. Magna treats it as a discipline.

## Verbatim Shailu

> *"Tools like Claude are like having an intern with Einstein-level intelligence but zero institutional memory. If the output is poor, it is because your context was poor."* [[strategic-investment-readiness-workshop]]

## The applied teaching

Shailu warns founders that **investors are now using AI to tear apart revenue models and market positioning**. The counter-strategy: proactively provide investors with specific prompts that ensure the AI evaluates your business on actual data, not hallucinated assumptions. *Bring your own context, or have someone else's hallucinated version stick.*
