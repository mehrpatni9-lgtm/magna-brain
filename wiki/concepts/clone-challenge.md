**TL;DR:** Shailu's strategy-workshop opening hook — *"If your clone was in this very room, how would you let others know what's the difference between you and the original?"* The strategy question collapses to: what makes you unmistakably the original? One of the strongest content hooks in the brain.

## Why it matters for Magna

This is a Shailu trademark move — open with a provocation, not a slide. The question reframes "what is your strategy?" into "what is your *identity in the market*?" — which is what strategy actually answers.

Highly postable. The phrase carries on its own and works as both a hook (top of post) and a closer (the question you leave the reader sitting with). Pairs naturally with [[magna-pillar-strategy]] content.

## Verbatim Shailu (live)

> *"If your clone was in this very room — how would you let others know what's the difference between you and the original? What would you do in your behaviors, in your actions, in your visible demonstration that you are the original and the other one is a clone?"* [[shailu-live-workshop-transcripts]]

## Why this lands

Three reasons the question works:

1. **It invokes a vivid scene.** Two of you in a room. Visceral.
2. **It shifts the unit of analysis from product to demonstration.** Strategy is what shows up *in behaviour*, not what's in the deck.
3. **It is uncomfortable.** Most founders cannot answer it specifically.

## How Shailu uses it

He opens the strategy session with this. It earns the attention before any framework appears. *"Every startup, enterprise, entrepreneur, product, and solution faces the clone challenge every day."*
