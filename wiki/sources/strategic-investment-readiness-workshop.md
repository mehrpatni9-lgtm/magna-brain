## What this source is

A workshop handout written and taught by Shailu on Strategic Investment Readiness. Distinct from his philosophical writing — this is **Shailu in the room, being tactical and direct**. Reveals how he shifts register when teaching practitioners versus when writing for reflection.

## Faithful extraction — frameworks introduced

- **Mindset of Leverage** — *"Seek investment when you do not need it. If you approach a firm out of desperation, you have already lost."* Power lives in not being thirsty. See [[mindset-of-leverage]].
- **Five-Question Investment Readiness Framework** — Scaling, Vision, Numbers, Valuation, Process. See [[five-question-investment-readiness]].
- **Internal Portfolio Review** — view your own company the way a VC views a portfolio (sector splits, track records, financial projections).
- **"Sleep on Horses"** — original Shailu phrase for entrepreneurial calm under high-pressure motion. See [[sleep-on-horses]].
- **The Three C's of Founder Balance** — Content, Community, Calendar (NOT to be confused with Magna's product-level 3 C's). See [[three-cs-founder-balance]].
- **The Filtered Pipeline** — *"Casting a wide net is a sign of a weak strategy."*
- **BCG Self-Categorization** — pre-empt the investor's matrix; align your narrative with their language before walking in.
- **Context Engineering** — the "Intelligent Intern" metaphor for AI: *"Tools like Claude are like having an intern with Einstein-level intelligence but zero institutional memory."* See [[context-engineering]].
- **Industry Experience as a Moat** — uses Examity and Eric S. Yuan (Zoom) as examples.
- **The Demo Hazard** — *"If you cannot manage a digital demo, an investor will assume you cannot manage their capital."*
- **Investor Type Comparison** — institutional vs. angels/F&F. Sharp framing: *"the fools in the room"* (deliberately blunt teaching move).

## What this source reveals about Shailu

He is more direct in the classroom than on paper. Uses personal examples without apology. Assigns exercises, not just principles. Respects operational detail (a broken Zoom link is a signal). Teaches AI as a context problem, not a technology problem.
