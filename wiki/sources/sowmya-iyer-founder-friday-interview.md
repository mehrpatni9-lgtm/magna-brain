**Faithful extraction from the ~25-min Founder Fridays interview with Sowmya Iyer (conducted by Mehr Patni). ASR mishears corrected; logistics break trimmed.**

## On "marketing that doesn't look like marketing"

Pull versus push. When a brand stands for something and people resonate, they come to it — that's pull, marketing that doesn't feel like marketing. The moment a brand pushes itself in your face, you get skeptics and credibility questions. What you stand for, how you blend in, and how useful you are in daily life decides whether you attract people or have to sell to them.

## On her edge (20+ years in digital)

Began at 16 running a .com portal. The last 18 months of AI made her last 13 years feel slow — yet she's more prepared while it shocks many companies. Experience = knowing how to adapt and adopt new tech. "Everybody is in this rut that we need to catch up." She isn't shocked by change.

## On the traditional agency model

It bills hours, ignores outcomes, is people-driven. Search, buying, and customer understanding have changed forever. Marketing must be system-led, not campaign-led. DViO moved from a digital marketing company to a growth operating system — "systems, not services." Data lets her design an entire customer experience; a system identifies the customer and lets automation talk to the brand.

## On the 2017 buyout

Cost a lot of money; taught her to trust an entrepreneur's gut. No business background; mortgaged her own house to buy out partners. Every consultant said no. "Something unique told me to do it" — defied logic. Conviction from authenticity means betting everything. Believing her gut is her biggest learning.

## On leadership

Fan of Simon Sinek. "Leaders eat last." They inspire and see more potential in people than the people see in themselves — unlocking potential is paramount. Need a clear vision: "one leg in the future, one leg in the present" — start from where your people are. Leaders eat last = the higher purpose before self.

## On Harvard OPM

"We're the sum total of the people we surround ourselves with." Entrepreneurs become a frog in the well, limited by vision. OPM expanded hers — meeting bigger entrepreneurs, understanding their thinking, exposure beyond her markets. "Increase your surface area for opportunities to come."

## On being a woman in agency ownership

Her gender never occurred to her while building; the world rewarded meritocracy. Self-funded; never raised capital. Never faced discrimination — even in the GCC since 2015, presenting to an all-male Sharia-compliant board 10 years ago who remain clients. "Women tend to identify so much with their own gender that they ruin it for themselves." Acknowledges real upliftment needed in tier-2/3 and rural India, but in advertising/media/martech/AI: "a woman-run company with no client ever having discriminated against me."

## On upbringing (Tamil Brahmin)

Education highly valued — marriage secondary; no boy/girl discrimination. Cites Indra Nooyi and TCS's Chandrasekaran. That value drew her back to academia at Harvard. Her mother: B.A. economics, financially independent, supported family 10 years, married at 33 ("unheard of"). "It comes from there. Generation after generation."

## Superpower wish

To be in multiple places at once — "you get so much more done when you're physically present somewhere."

## This or That

- Boardroom or classroom → **Boardroom**
- Work forever or retire early → **work-life integration**; freedom is the entrepreneur's biggest gift
- Solo founder or co-founder → **Solo founder**
- Investor meeting or client dinner → **Client**
