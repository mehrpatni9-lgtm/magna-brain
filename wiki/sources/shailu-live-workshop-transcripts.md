## Faithful extraction — frameworks per workshop

### Strategy

- **One-line definition (Shailu's own)**: *"Strategy is simply the choices that you are making and how you are communicating — first to your team, then to your market, then to your ecosystem — in that order."*
- Distilled: *"Focus to say no to things that will distract you, and the discipline to do things that you must do to propel forward."*
- **The Clone Challenge** opener — *"If your clone was in this very room — how would you let others know what's the difference?"* See [[clone-challenge]].
- **The Magnet Triangle (Business DNA)** — Customer Needs → Value Addition → Channels → Pricing → Mission. See [[magnet-triangle]].
- **The Strategy Communication Gap** — *"Top floor, not the shop floor."* Strategy formulation vs strategy communication.
- **The Customer Quadrant** — few/many things × few/many customers. Pricing + positioning matrix.
- Confirmed [[creation-acceleration-transition]] verbatim.

### Negotiation

- **Core reframe**: *"Negotiation is not a game where somebody wins and somebody loses. Look at it as a process."* See [[superpowers-of-negotiation]].
- *"You're dealing with people in a company, not the company."*
- **3 superpowers**: Clarity of preparation; Clarity of assumptions; Predicting outcomes.
- **The overconfidence problem**: *"Stop rating yourself. Rate the quality of the transaction."*
- **The forgotten permission**: *"You don't need to finish the deal all the time."*
- **The inevitability**: *"Negotiation is not an option. It is imposed on you whether you like it or not."*

### Sales

- **The single strongest line**: *"The Magna world of sales does not teach sales. The Magna world of sales enables buying."* See [[magna-enables-buying]].
- **The dating analogy**: *"Asking out somebody on a date is a sale. The reason they go on a date with you is branding. Setting up the whole environment is marketing."*
- **3 objectives**: *"Conquering the heart space, mind space, eventually the purse of the customer"* — in that order.
- **Outer ring**: Identity → Experience → Engagement → Loyalty.
- *"It's like sugar in your coffee. You feel the flavor but you don't get to see it."*
- **Inner triangle**: Strategy → Design → Communication. *"Communicate in the frequency of the customer, not yours."*
- **4 P's in a contract**: Price · Product · Positioning · Promotion. *"Most people offer vagueness. We give specificity."*
- **The data flood problem** — 75,000+ data footprints per customer (in 2017). Identity-first, not noise-first.

## What this source reveals about Shailu's teaching pattern

1. Opens with a provocation, not a slide.
2. Humanises everything (sales as dating).
3. Inverts the expected (sales → buying; strategy → saying no).
4. Uses one-line anchors that travel.
5. Sequential, not scattered.
