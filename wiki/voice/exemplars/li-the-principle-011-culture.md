**Title:** MAGNA PRINCIPLE #011: "Culture isn't what you say. It's what you tolerate."

**Body:**

Every culture speech a CEO gives is immediately tested by the next thing they let slide.

The team that watches you close your eyes to the high-performer who bullies his reports? They've just learned what your values actually are.

Not the poster on the wall — the behaviour at the edge.

Culture compounds. Every tolerance creates permission. Every permission creates expectation.

—

What's the one thing you wish you hadn't tolerated for too long?

**Why it's an exemplar:** Short Dalio-register principle. Concrete scene ("high-performer who bullies his reports") does heavy lifting. The phrase "behaviour at the edge" is the Magna-isable distinction.
