# Magna voice

**One line:** Quiet confidence. Depth of thought. Enduring credibility.

Magna is not a hypey brand. It is a grounded, intellectual brand that sounds
like a practitioner with 25 years of scars, not a marketer with a funnel.
Shailu is the voice of LinkedIn. Magna is the voice of Instagram. Both are
the same brand with different registers.

## The six voices (Tone Compass)

Magna maps every post to exactly one of six reference voices. The voice is
chosen by the **content series**, not by mood.

| Voice | Register | Used for |
|---|---|---|
| **Naval Ravikant** | Dense, compressed, one big idea, no fluff. Short sentences that feel like they took years to compress. The quote that gets saved, not liked. | `#MagnaMondays` (IG Monday) |
| **Ray Dalio** | Numbered principles. Radical transparency about failures. The voice of pattern recognition distilled. | `#ThePrinciple` (LI Tuesday), `#DigitalLectures` (LI Sat bi-weekly) |
| **Seth Godin** | Provocative but never loud. Remarkable thinking about ordinary things. Two-sentence observation that rewires how you see the market. | `#TheSharpTake` (LI Thursday), `#WisdomWednesdays` (IG Wednesday) |
| **Simon Sinek** | Start with the human. Emotion before the lesson. The why before the what. | `#FounderFridays` (IG Friday), `#BehindTheBuild` (LI Sat alt weeks) |
| **Daniel Pink** | Motivational but grounded in research. Autonomy. Mastery. Purpose. Makes founders feel the meaning behind the work. | `#InspirationWeekends` (IG Sunday) |
| **Tony Robbins** | Energy and momentum, restrained. **Never the default.** Only for event launches, membership opens, community celebrations. | CTAs & event posts only |

## Non-negotiable tone rules

1. **No selling on LinkedIn. Ever.** Shailu posts as a practitioner. The
   product sells itself when the thinking is undeniable.
2. **Every post ends with one thing:** a question, a shareable truth, or a
   specific challenge. Never a CTA like "DM me" or "book a call."
3. **Start with the hook, not the setup.** First line is the one that gets
   the scroll to stop. No warm-ups.
4. **Specific over abstract.** "$10M → $50M" beats "scaling." "The 3rd
   Strategy Roundtable in Dubai" beats "our events."
5. **No jargon, no buzzwords.** "Thought leadership," "synergy," "disrupt,"
   "unlock," "10x" — banned. Replace with plain words.
6. **Quietly certain, never loud.** Magna doesn't shout. Magna states.
7. **Length follows series, not instinct.** See each series' spec in
   `[[concepts/content-series-*]]`.

## Worldview (the ground every post sits on)

- Founders hit walls because they avoid the thinking their situation demands, not because they lack information.
- The $10M → $50M gap is a thinking shift, not a tactical one.
- Culture is what you tolerate, not what you say.
- Capital is a tool. Judgment is the multiplier.
- Strategy is easy to buy. Clarity on which problem is actually the problem is the hard part — and that's what Magna is built for.

If a draft contradicts one of these, the draft is wrong.

## Good Magna post checklist

- First line alone is worth the post.
- Exactly one idea. Not two.
- Ends on a provocation, never a sale.
- Remove Magna framing → thinking still holds.
- A $10M–$50M founder would screenshot it before 9am.

## Magna never writes

- Tagline marketing copy ("Unlock your potential")
- Listicles — unless numbered in `#ThePrinciple`
- CTA closes ("Book a call")
- Humblebrag stats
- Hustle framing
- Motivational posters
