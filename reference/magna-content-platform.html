#ThePrinciple

Every Tuesday · Ray Dalio voice
#ThePrinciple 004Every Tuesday
One operating truth. Unpacked with depth.

MAGNA PRINCIPLE #004: "Never negotiate a deal you're not prepared to walk away from."

Most founders enter negotiations hoping to close. That hope costs them — every time.

When you need the deal, the other side can feel it. Your body language changes. Your asks shrink before you even make them. You accept terms you'd never accept if the pressure was off.

Preparation isn't just knowing your numbers. It's knowing — genuinely knowing — what you'll do if this doesn't close. Your next best option isn't a backup plan. It's your power source.

The founders who win in negotiation aren't the most aggressive. They're the most unattached.

—

What's a deal you've regretted because you needed it too much? Drop it in the comments — I'll tell you the one thing most founders in that position get wrong.

#ThePrinciple 011Every Tuesday
One operating truth. Unpacked with depth.

MAGNA PRINCIPLE #011: "Culture isn't what you say. It's what you tolerate."

Every culture speech a CEO gives is immediately tested by the next thing they let slide.

The team that watches you close your eyes to the high-performer who bullies his reports? They've just learned what your values actually are. Not the poster on the wall — the behaviour at the edge.

Culture compounds. Every tolerance creates permission. Every permission creates expectation.

—

What's the one thing you wish you hadn't tolerated for too long?
